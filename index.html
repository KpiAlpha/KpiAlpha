PHROZEN FOR OVER 40 YEARS

MANLY DEEDS, SCHOLARSHIP, AND LOVE FOR ALL MANKIND

Following the principles of its Jewels, the Kappa Pi Chapter of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity was chartered at the College of William & Mary on May 31, 1975 by Timothy Earnest Allmond, Jr., Nathaniel Adeoluwa Folarin, William Lorenzo Jackson, Jr., Sheldon Jerome Johnson, John Oscar Little, Jr., Adeyemo Folusho Olanrewaju, Ronald Harvey Smoot, and Willie George Webb Walton. Though limited in numbers since its inception, the brothers of the Kappa Pi chapter have been dedicated to service on campus and in the Williamsburg community.

Throughout our history, brothers have served as mentors at the Chicahominy Youth Center, D.J. Montague Elementary School, the Williamsburg Head Start program, and Toano and Berkley Middle Schools as a part of the Go to High School, Go to College Campaign. Additionally, brothers of our chapter have served with Housing Partnerships and Adopt-A-Highway, and organized our “A Voteless People is a Hopeless People” voter registration campaigns. These activities can prove to be very demanding for a chapter which normally functions with less than ten brothers. As a result, brothers must put aside personal agendas for the good of the fraternity. Rhode Scholar and University of Georgia English professor, Dr. Andrew Zawacki, explained this well during his days as an undergraduate, “Most of my life I had been pretty much an individual. However, the most important lesson that I learned as a member of Alpha Phi Alpha is that unity does not have to mean conformity, and that it is healthy to come together with others if you believe in a worthy cause.”

To the youths who look onward and upward towards the light, we invite you to explore the rich history of our chapter, to examine our current service initiatives, and to get involved with the brothers of the Kappa Pi Chapter.
